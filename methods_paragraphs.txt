BAYES' THEOREM — PREVALENCE-ADJUSTED PPV FOR OUR COHORT
================================================================================

Our cohort ARDS prevalence: P = 2,419 / 19,052 = 12.7%

Formula:
  PPV_adjusted = (Se x P) / (Se x P + (1 - Sp) x (1 - P))

Inputs:
  Se = 0.52  (validation cohort)
  Sp = 0.69  (validation cohort)
  P  = 0.127 (our cohort)

Calculation:
  Numerator   = 0.52 x 0.127                     = 0.0660
  Denominator = 0.52 x 0.127 + 0.31 x 0.873      = 0.0660 + 0.2706 = 0.3366
  PPV_adj     = 0.0660 / 0.3366                  = 19.6% ~ 20%

Verification at validation-cohort prevalence (53%):
  (0.52 x 0.53) / (0.52 x 0.53 + 0.31 x 0.47) = 0.2756 / 0.4213 = 65.4% ~ 66% [CHECKS OUT]

IMPORTANT CAVEAT: Hochberg et al. explicitly note that Se and Sp are secondary outcomes
and likely underestimated, because the clinical adjudication reference did not require
sustained hypoxemia — some classifier-negative patients may have had true ARDS that
resolved before the 12-24h persistence window. The paper states: "If true negatives are
underestimated, as we expect, sensitivity is also likely underestimated." The ~20%
adjusted PPV is therefore a lower bound; the true value is likely modestly higher.

DRAFT LIMITATIONS PARAGRAPH FOR CLAIRE
================================================================================

The persistent AHRF cohort was identified using an EHR-based classifier validated by
Hochberg et al. (Crit Care Med 2026;54:654-660). In the independent 90-patient validation
cohort — drawn from a geographically distinct academic medical ICU with 53% ARDS
prevalence — the classifier's positive predictive value (PPV) was 66% (95% CI 50-81%).
However, PPV is prevalence-dependent. The classifier's sensitivity and specificity in the
validation cohort were 52% (95% CI 38-67%) and 69% (95% CI 55-82%), respectively.
Applying Bayes' theorem, the expected PPV in our cohort — where the base rate of
persistent moderate-to-severe ARDS among mechanically ventilated patients is substantially
lower (~12.7%; 2,419 of 19,052 MV encounters) — is approximately 20%. This means a
meaningful proportion of patients in the persistent AHRF cohort may not have met full
Berlin ARDS criteria at the time of their index hypoxemia. Hochberg et al. note that
classifier sensitivity is likely underestimated because the clinical adjudication reference
standard did not require sustained hypoxemia; accordingly, the 20% estimate should be
interpreted as approximate. Critically, misclassification of individual ARDS status does
not bias the primary analysis, which estimates attending-level variation in tidal-volume
practice across all patients meeting hypoxemia criteria — a population enriched for but
not restricted to Berlin-definition ARDS.

The 66% PPV above refers solely to the ARDS classifier performance and should not be
conflated with two separate validation figures: the 93% agreement reported for the
attending-assignment algorithm (chart-review validation; [citation]) and the 96%
recent-year accuracy of that same algorithm ([citation]).

================================================================================
DRAFT METHODS PARAGRAPH FOR CLAIRE
================================================================================

COVID-19 status was defined as a binary indicator at the hospitalization level.
A hospitalization was classified as COVID-19–positive if a positive SARS-CoV-2
nucleic acid amplification or antigen test was recorded in the microbiology
record within 14 days before or on the date of first intubation for that
encounter. All hospitalizations without a matching positive test result within
this window were classified as COVID-19–negative (reference category). This
binary indicator was held constant across all patient-ventilator-days within a
hospitalization and was included as a patient-level covariate in all primary
mixed-effects logistic regression models to adjust for systematic differences in
LTVV adherence associated with active SARS-CoV-2 infection.
